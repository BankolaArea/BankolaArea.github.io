Historical Timeline of Coal Price Regulation in India

1945: Colliery Control Order

Empowered the Central Government to fix coal prices under the Defence of India Rules.

"The Colliery Control Order, 1945, was enacted to regulate coal prices during wartime exigencies."
[1] Essential Commodities Act, 1955

1994: Last Government-Fixed Price Revision

Final comprehensive price revision by the government on 17 June 1994.

"The government last revised coal prices administratively on June 17, 1994."
[2] NITI Aayog Report, 2021 (p. 98)

2000: Full Deregulation

The Colliery Control Order, 2000 came into effect, transferring pricing autonomy to CIL.

"The Colliery Control Order 2000... removed the government’s authority to fix coal prices."
[3] Ministry of Coal

2005: E-Auction Introduction

CIL launched e-auctions for market-based price discovery.

"E-auction mechanism was introduced to ensure transparency and price fairness."
[4] CIL Annual Report 2022-23 (p. 49)

2020: National Coal Index (NCI)

Launched to track coal price trends and guide auctions.

"NCI provides a transparent benchmark for coal pricing in commercial auctions."
[5] Ministry of Coal

2024: Coal Reforms 3.0

Focus on coal gasification, underground mining, and non-power sector allocations.

"Coal Reforms 3.0 incentivize coal-to-chemical projects with 50% revenue rebates."
[6] PIB Release, Jan 2024
