Modernization and Technological Adoption in Coal India Limited: Challenges and Strategic Responses

Introduction

Coal India Limited (CIL), the world’s largest coal producer, is at a critical juncture. The push for efficiency, productivity, and sustainability is driving CIL to modernize its mining operations and adopt new technologies—but numerous challenges remain.1

Key Challenges to Modernization and Technological Adoption

1. Legacy Mines and Low Mechanization

  • High Share of Manual Mines: Over 60% of CIL’s underground mines still use conventional, labor-intensive methods.
    “Underground production as a percentage of total output has dropped to 5%, despite constituting 43% of the workforce.” (Coal India Ltd Annual Report 2022-23, p. 32)2
  • Low Productivity: Manual methods yield 0.52 tonnes per man shift in underground mines compared to 7.38 tonnes in opencast mines.
    “The productivity in opencast mines is much higher due to mechanized methods and use of high capacity equipment.” (NITI Aayog, p. 102)1

2. Workforce and Cultural Challenges

  • Skill Gap: Many CIL workers are unfamiliar with advanced mining equipment, requiring large-scale reskilling.
    “Absence of skilled manpower is a hurdle in faster adoption of high-end technology in mining.” (NITI Aayog, p. 106)1
  • Resistance to Change: Workforce and unions sometimes oppose mechanization due to fears of job loss.
    “There is resistance to mechanization from the workforce and trade unions due to the fear of job losses.” (NITI Aayog, p. 106)1

3. Financial and Regulatory Bottlenecks

  • High Capital Cost: Imported machinery like continuous miners is expensive, and returns from underground mining are often insufficient.
    “High capital investment and low productivity in underground mines make them financially unviable for large-scale mechanization.” (NITI Aayog, p. 103)1
  • Project Delays: Land acquisition, forest/environmental clearances, and social resistance delay new technology projects.
    “As of March 2023, 119 coal projects...have been delayed due to land acquisition, forest clearance, and law & order issues.” (CIL Annual Report 2022-23, p. 35)2

4. Digitalization and Data Challenges

  • Legacy IT Systems: Integrating new digital solutions (ERP, real-time monitoring) with old systems is slow.
    “Implementation of digital solutions faces challenges due to legacy systems and lack of interoperability across subsidiaries.” (CIL Annual Report 2022-23, p. 103)2

Modernization Initiatives and Strategic Responses

1. Mechanization & Indigenous Technology

  • Continuous Miner and Longwall Mining: CIL has deployed 38 continuous miners and several longwall sets, but still far short of need.
    “Adoption of continuous miners, longwall mining, and surface miners has improved productivity in select mines.” (CIL Annual Report 2022-23, p. 28)2
  • Indigenization Drive: In December 2023, India’s first indigenous continuous miner was commissioned at Jhanjra mine (ECL) in collaboration with Gainwell Engineering.
    “ECL has achieved a milestone by introducing the first indigenously built continuous miner at Jhanjra Project.” (The Hindu Business Line, Dec 19, 2023)3
Fact: “Jhanjra’s indigenously built continuous miner is expected to cut import dependence and cost for underground mechanisation.” (The Hindu Business Line, Dec 19, 2023)3

2. Digitalization & Automation

  • IoT and Automation: Sensors and IoT solutions for real-time equipment monitoring have been piloted in SECL and MCL.
    “IoT based monitoring solutions and remote-controlled machines are being piloted in South Eastern Coalfields.” (NITI Aayog, p. 104)1
  • Drone Surveys: Drones are being used for land surveys, mine planning, and environmental monitoring in 15+ mines.
    “Drone-based surveys are conducted in several mines to improve planning and compliance.” (CIL Annual Report 2022-23, p. 103)2

3. Skill Development & Change Management

  • Training Initiatives: Over 1,500 employees trained for new underground mining systems in 2022-23.
    “Extensive training and skill development programmes have been launched for mechanized mining and digital systems.” (CIL Annual Report 2022-23, p. 59)2

4. Clean Coal and Environmental Technologies

  • Coal Gasification & Clean Energy: CIL has committed ₹3,000 crore for coal gasification and clean coal research.
    “CIL has earmarked Rs 3,000 crore for setting up coal gasification projects to promote cleaner coal utilization.” (CIL Annual Report 2022-23, p. 16)2

Outlook

CIL targets 1 billion tonnes of production by FY2025-26, with a major share from highly mechanized mines and digital operations.
“CIL has set a target to reach 1 BT production by FY25-26, with an increasing share from opencast and mechanized mines.” (CIL Annual Report 2022-23, p. 7)2

However, true modernization requires ongoing investment, strong training programs, supply chain upgrades, and collaborative change management.
